Dr. Hyduk

Our Trusted Captain
CDABO Past President
Loves to Ski & Golf

Dr. Hyduk brings over 40 years of experience and American Board-Certification to the Ortho team. His reputation for outstanding patient care, contributions to research, and commitment to education has been recognized across medical disciplines.

Growing up in southeast Michigan, Dr. Hyduk graduated from the University of Detroit Mercy School of Dentistry. He participated in a rotating internship at Sinai Hospital, Detroit, and completed an orthodontic residency at Fairleigh Dickinson University in Hackensack, New Jersey. At the age of 23, he became the youngest certified orthodontist to practice in the United States.

His professional engagement had led him to affiliations with the American Association of Orthodontists, the Great Lakes Association of Orthodontists, and the Michigan Association of Orthodontists. In 2012, he was elected President of the College of Diplomates of the American Board of Orthodontics – one of the field’s most prestigious organizations.

Orthodontics as it relates to psychological well-being has motivated Dr. Hyduk to perform extensive research. Partnering with a number of hospitals and universities including Fairleigh Dickinson University, New Jersey, Montefiore Hospital, The Bronx, NY, and the University of Michigan, Ann Arbor, MI, he has helped the medical community better understand the delicate relationship between degrees of facial deformities, patient self-esteem, and the role of the orthodontist. It is this experience that has expanded his expertise beyond basic orthodontic treatment, and influenced how he approaches care for all patients.

Dr. Hyduk is currently active on medical staffs at William Beaumont, St. Joseph Mercy Oakland and Crittenton Hospitals. He is a senior team member of the Ian Jackson Craniofacial & Cleft Palate Clinic with the department of neurosurgery at Beaumont Royal Oak. He is also an associate professor of the Oakland University William Beaumont School of Medicine in Rochester, MI, and maintains a residency teaching position at St. Joseph Mercy Oakland. He continues to teach and lecture both nationally & internationally.

Outside of orthodontics, Dr. Hyduk enjoys traveling, golf, skiing, and spending as much time as possible with his wife and 2 kids.
