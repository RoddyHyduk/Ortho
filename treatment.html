For Kids

Age Seven

The American Association of Orthodontics recommends an initial orthodontic check-up no later than age 7.

By this time, your child’s first permanent molars and incisors will have probably started to emerge, and Dr. Hyduk will be able to spot any subtle problems with the growth of the jaw.

One of the main benefits of beginning treatment for your child at a young age is the ability to pursue Two Phase Treatment: an orthodontic approach that addresses the proper relationship between jaws, in addition to the correct alignment of teeth.

Headgear

Phase 1 Treatment

Focusing on Jaw Relationships, we guide the growth of jaw bones to support teeth and correct skeletal abnormalities through the use of headgear.


Once your child’s facial growth is completed (around age 12), the window of opportunity for impactful Phase 1 treatment closes. Corrections to jaw alignment later in life will rely on complete nonsurgical remodeling or jaw surgery, so early childhood treatment is a much preferred alternative.

Braces

Phase 2 Treatment

Aligning Teeth, we move permanent teeth into final positions using braces and aligners.


Phase 2 treatment is what most people think of when they consider orthodontics, and seldom begins earlier than age 11, as it relies on the presence of fully-erupted permanent teeth.

If your child will benefit from Two-Phase Treatment, Dr. Hyduk will let you know during your initial free exam.

For Adults

There persists a common misconception that successful orthodontic treatment isn’t possible for adults, yet the truth is that healthy teeth can be moved at any age. Orthodontic forces move teeth for adults the same as they do for children.

According to the American Association of Orthodontics, 1 in 5 patients receiving orthodontic care today are over the age of 21.

The main difference between adult and child orthodontics is that children’s jawbones are still developing. For adults, treatment plans may include the possibility of orthognathic surgery, in which the jawbones are surgically aligned since facial development has ceased. Additional factors that orthodontists consider when working with adults include:

Gum or bone loss (periodontal disease)
Worn, prosthetically restored and/or missing teeth
Incomplete orthodontic treatment from one’s youth

As a board-certified orthodontist, Dr. Hyduk will best assess what treatment options are ideal for you, and he will work with your primary dentist to ensure the best possible results.

Please note that veneers, crowns, and other bonding procedures are rarely considered acceptable treatments for teeth alignment, and thus should be considered in tandem and not instead of orthodontic treatment.

Benefits of Adult Orthodontics

Beyond creating a more confident smile, orthodontic treatment for adults can lead to a healthier mouth. Orthodontic care can help you reduce the likelihood of tooth decay and gum disease by making it easier to brush and floss. And in recent years, a growing number of orthodontists have begun to uncover new links between oral health and the overall health of your body.

To determine if treatment is right for you, call our office to schedule your initial complimentary exam. No matter your age, we believe that your smile deserves to be as beautiful as you are.

TMJ Disorders

Your temporomandibular joints (TMJs) connect your lower jawbone to your skull and are two of the most complex joints in the body – capable of moving in infinite combinations that allow for speaking, chewing, swallowing, and yawning.

TMJ disorders occur when your jaw muscles and joints function improperly, leading to recurrent pain and spasms. Causes for TMJ vary greatly, though often are the result of facial trauma, arthritis, or severe stress. Millions of Americans suffer from undiagnosed TMJ disorders, and the highest incidence rates occur in females ages 20-44.

Common Symptoms Include:

Frequent Headaches, Neck-aches
Soreness in Jaw
Dull, Aching Facial Pain
Jaw Joing Clicking and Popping
Clenching or Griding of Teeth
Locked Jaw or Limited Opening of Mouth

If you notice abnormal, recurring pain in your jaw, we recommend you schedule a free examination. Dr. Hyduk will help you determine if you suffer from a TMJ disorder and will work with you to create a customized treatment plan to bring you relief.

Sleep Apnea

Sleep apnea is a medical condition in which breathing is temporarily obstructed during sleep. A patient suffering from sleep apnea stops breathing hundreds of times during the course of one night, and each breathing pause lasts between 10-20 seconds. Each time breathing stops, the lack of oxygen causes the body to temporarily wake up, repeatedly interrupting sleep for all inflicted patients.

Left untreated, sleep apnea can lead to fatigue, headaches, and even high blood pressure, diabetes, and risk of heart failure. More than 18 million Americans suffer from sleep apnea today, many of whom proceed without treatment.

Common Symptoms Include:

  • Chronic Snoring
  • Excessive Fatique
  • Waking up Unrested
  • Tension Headaches
  • Falling Asleep Driving, Watching TV, etc.

If you suspect you suffer from sleep apnea, consider scheduling a free examination with Dr. Hyduk. Treatment plans vary greatly depending on individual needs, though often involve the use of oral devices that an orthodontist can prescribe to prevent throat blockage. Dr. Hyduk will assist with your diagnosis and will work with your doctor to improve your sleep.
